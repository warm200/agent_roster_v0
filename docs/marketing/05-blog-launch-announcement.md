# Why We Built OpenRoster

*The agent marketplace that thinks in combinations, not capabilities.*

---

There are a lot of AI agent marketplaces now. Most of them work the same way: browse a list, pick one, grant it access, hope it does the thing. If you need two agents to work together, that's your problem.

We think that model is broken. Not because individual agents are bad — they're often impressive — but because the valuable unit isn't the agent. It's the combination.

## The real workflow isn't one agent deep

Think about how your morning actually works.

You check email. You triage what's urgent. You look at your calendar and figure out what's protected and what can move. You pull together a brief of what happened overnight so you can walk into your first meeting informed.

That's not one task. It's three tasks in sequence, with shared context, producing a combined outcome. An inbox-triage agent does one piece. A calendar-guard agent does another. A daily-brief agent does a third. But the value only shows up when they work as a loop.

That's what OpenRoster builds around: **bundles**.

## What bundles actually are

A bundle is a group of agents composed into a single operating workspace. You pick the agents, shape the combination, and launch it as one unit.

We ship three templates to start:

- **Morning Ops** — inbox triage, calendar guard, daily brief
- **Founder Inbox** — priority sorter, reply drafter, follow-up tracker
- **Weekly Review** — notes digester, decision summary, next-week planner

But the real product is the bundles *you* compose. Over time, your OpenRoster becomes less about browsing a catalog and more about keeping a set of combinations that fit your actual routines.

## Preview before commitment

Every agent on OpenRoster has a free preview chat. Not a demo video. Not a feature list. An actual sandbox conversation where you can test its tone, logic, and boundaries.

We built this because the biggest friction in agent adoption isn't price — it's uncertainty. People don't know how an agent will behave until they've used it, and by then they've already committed.

Preview solves this. Understand the agent first. Buy runtime later.

## Risk as a feature, not fine print

Every agent on the platform carries a risk label: Low, Medium, or High. Each label comes with specific capability disclosures — chat-only, file access, network access, shell access.

We wrote these labels for normal people, not compliance lawyers. The goal is inspectability: you should be able to look at a bundle's risk profile and understand exactly what it can and can't do before you commit money or workspace access.

We think trust erodes when risk is buried in fine print. So we put it on the surface.

## The pricing model

Agents are free to collect. All of them. Preview chat is free too.

You only pay when you launch a bundle in a managed runtime environment. One credit equals one successful launch. Failed starts are refunded automatically.

Three runtime modes depending on what you need:

- **Run** — bounded manual session for testing
- **Warm Standby** — wake on Telegram message, recover from last state
- **Always On** — persistent workspace that stays live

The split is simple: discovery is free, execution is paid. We think this is the right boundary because browsing and understanding should never have a tollgate.

## What's live today

50+ agents across inbox, calendar, docs, and team workflows. Telegram integration for real handoff — not a toy webhook, but actual operational pairing with status, logs, and downloadable artifacts.

Risk labels on every agent. Free preview chat on every agent. Bundle composition with combined risk profiles.

No waitlist. No invite code. Just go.
