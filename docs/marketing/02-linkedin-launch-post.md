**We just launched OpenRoster. Here's what we built and why.**

Every AI agent marketplace sells you one tool at a time. A calendar bot here, an inbox sorter there. Then you're left stitching outputs together, remembering sequences, managing handoffs between disconnected agents.

That's not automation — that's overhead wearing a better skin.

We built OpenRoster around a different premise: **the valuable unit is the combination, not the individual agent.**

OpenRoster is a managed-agent marketplace where you compose *bundles* — groups of agents that work as a single operating loop. Morning Ops. Founder Inbox. Weekly Review. You pick the agents, shape the bundle, and launch it as one workspace.

Three things we got right that I haven't seen elsewhere:

**1. Preview before commitment.**
You can have a real conversation with any agent before you pay anything. Not a demo video. Not marketing copy. An actual sandbox chat so you understand how it thinks.

**2. Risk labels that normal people can read.**
Every agent ships with a plain-English risk profile. Low, Medium, High — with specific capability disclosures. No "grant access to everything" dialogs.

**3. Agents are free. Runtime is paid.**
Browse and collect 50+ agents at zero cost. You only pay when you launch a bundle in a managed sandbox. 1 credit = 1 launch. Failed starts are refunded automatically.

We're live now with 50+ agents across inbox, calendar, docs, and team workflows. Telegram integration for real handoff. Logs, status, and artifacts in one place.

No waitlist. No invite code.
