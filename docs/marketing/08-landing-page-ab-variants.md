# Landing Page A/B Hero Variants — OpenRoster.ai

> Test against current: "Compose bundles, not one-off tools" / "Build focused work bundles that run like a real workspace."

---

## Variant A: Workflow-First

**Kicker:** Your morning ops, on autopilot.

**Headline:** Stop wiring agents together. Compose a bundle and launch the whole workflow.

**Subhead:** OpenRoster is the managed-agent marketplace where the product is the combination. Pick agents, shape a bundle, preview before you pay, and run the workspace when the job is real.

**CTA:** Browse Agents | See How Bundles Work

**Thesis:** Leads with pain (manual coordination) and relief (bundles handle it). More action-oriented.

---

## Variant B: Outcome-First

**Kicker:** 50+ agents. Free to preview. Pay only to run.

**Headline:** Three agents. One operating loop. Done before standup.

**Subhead:** OpenRoster lets you compose agent bundles that handle your daily ops — inbox triage, calendar guard, daily brief — as a single managed workspace. Preview any agent first. Risk labels upfront. No mystery permissions.

**CTA:** Compose a Bundle | Explore the Catalog

**Thesis:** Leads with a concrete outcome (morning done before standup). More tangible and specific.

---

## Variant C: Trust-First

**Kicker:** Agent marketplace. Bundle-first. Risk-transparent.

**Headline:** The agent marketplace where you see everything before you pay for anything.

**Subhead:** Free preview chat with every agent. Plain-English risk labels on every bundle. Transparent pricing — agents are free, you pay only for managed runtime. No blanket permissions. No mystery behavior.

**CTA:** Preview an Agent Free | See the Risk Labels

**Thesis:** Leads with trust and transparency. Targets the skeptical buyer who's been burned by opaque AI tools.

---

## Testing Notes

- **Variant A** likely resonates with people already using multiple AI tools and frustrated by the integration tax.
- **Variant B** likely performs best with people who respond to concrete outcomes and specificity.
- **Variant C** likely wins with security-conscious buyers and enterprise-adjacent users.

Recommended test order: B → A → C (specificity tends to outperform abstraction in early-stage products).
