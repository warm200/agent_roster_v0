## Email 3: Power User Tips (Day 5)

**Subject:** Three things power users figured out first.

**Preview text:** Bundles > agents. Warm Standby > manual runs. Risk labels > trust.

---

Hey {first_name},

A few patterns we've seen from early users that are worth knowing:

**1. Think in bundles, not agents.**

The people getting the most value aren't collecting the most agents — they're maintaining 2-3 bundles that match real routines. Morning prep. Inbox loops. Weekly reviews. The bundle is the product.

**2. Warm Standby is the sweet spot.**

Most workflows aren't one-shot. They repeat. Warm Standby lets your bundle wake on a Telegram message and resume from where it left off — no cold start, no lost context. It's the right mode for anything you run more than once.

**3. Risk labels save you time.**

Before you add an agent to a bundle, check its risk badge. Low = chat-only. Medium = file access. High = shell + network. The bundle shows a combined risk profile so you know exactly what the whole workspace can do before you launch.

**One more thing:** We're building new bundles based on the compositions users create organically. If you've built something that works well for your workflow, we'd love to hear about it. Reply to this email.
